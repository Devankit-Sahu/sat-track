About SatTrack

This Web Application take the Input from User and Database for a desired Indian satellite and based on the inputs specified by the user, project the orbital positions on the Earth’s surface to generate sensor footprint.

Following are the inputs which can be provided by the user to the application to guide them for determining the imaging opportunities.
1) Coordinates of point of interest in terms of Latitude and Longitude on Earth.
2) Sensor within the payload.
3) Possible swath which can be imaged with the particular sensor.
4) Tilt of the satellite within the known agility of the platform.
5) Orbital parameters like semi-major axis, eccentricity, inclination, longitude of ascending node, argument of perigee and true anomaly based on the publicly available information in the form of two line elements (TLE)


Objective :

Objective is of finding the possibility or predict the time of imaging a particular object/place (Lat-Long) on Earth by an Earth observing satellite with given sensor specifications, orbit and attitude definitions under various Orbit Preturbations

Background :

Low Earth observation satellites orbits the Earth surface at an altitude of around 300 to 1000km in Sun synchronous polar orbit. The sensors are placed on the satellite platform to take pictures of the Earth’s surface. Users are interested in the opportunities when their area of interest is going to be imaged in order to meet their application requirement. This software based application enable them to know the imaging opportunities apriori for their planning. Agile satellite platforms have the capability to image certain area with tilts which is defined by revisit constraints. This application should be able to guide the user for imaging of their area of interest taking into account the tilt requirements. This information can be shared by them to satellite pass programming persons.
